The Sunlight Zone

Epipelagic Zone

This is the ocean's surface layer, home to 90% of all marine life. Here, sunlight is abundant, powering vast kelp forests and microscopic phytoplankton that generate over half of the Earth's oxygen.

Pressure: 1 - 20 atm
Temp: 12°C to 30°C
200 - 1000m

The Twilight Zone

Mesopelagic Zone

Sunlight rapidly fades here, leaving only a dim blue glow. Photosynthesis is impossible, and the inhabitants are bizarre. Many creatures here migrate to the surface at night to feed in the largest migration on Earth.

Pressure: Up to 100 atm
Temp: 4°C to 12°C
1000m+

The Midnight Zone

Bathypelagic Zone

Welcome to absolute dark. No sunlight ever reaches this depth. The water is near-freezing, and the pressure is crushing. Creatures here rely on bioluminescence—producing their own light—to capture prey and find mates.

Pressure: 100+ atm (Crushing)
Temp: 1°C to 4°C
